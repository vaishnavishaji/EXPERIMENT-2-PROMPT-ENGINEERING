# EXP-2-PROMPT-ENGINEERING-

## Aim: 
Comparative Analysis of different types of Prompting patterns and explain with Various Test Scenarios

Experiment:
Test and compare how different pattern models respond to various prompts (broad or unstructured) versus basic prompts (clearer and more refined) across multiple scenarios. 
Analyze the quality, accuracy, and depth of the generated responses.


## Algorithm:
## Objective

To analyze how different prompting patterns influence AI-generated responses in terms of accuracy, clarity, depth, relevance, structure, and completeness.

## Test Scenario 1: Broad / Unstructured Prompt

Prompt: “What is Artificial Intelligence?”

Response

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the ability of machines or computers to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. These tasks include learning, problem-solving, understanding language, and decision-making. AI is used in many areas like robots, virtual assistants, and automation systems.

## Test Scenario 2: Basic Clear Prompt

Prompt: “Explain Artificial Intelligence in simple words suitable for a beginner.”

Response

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a technology that allows machines to think and act like humans. For example, when you use voice assistants like Siri or Google Assistant, they understand your voice and respond. AI helps computers learn from data and improve over time without being directly programmed for every task.

## Test Scenario 3: Detailed Structured Prompt

Prompt:
“Explain Artificial Intelligence. Include:

Definition
Types
Applications
Advantages and limitations
Future scope”
Response

Definition:
Artificial Intelligence is a branch of computer science that enables machines to simulate human intelligence.

Types:

Narrow AI: Performs specific tasks (e.g., chatbots)
General AI: Can perform any intellectual task (still theoretical)
Super AI: More intelligent than humans (future concept)

Applications:

Healthcare (diagnosis systems)
Finance (fraud detection)
Education (smart tutoring)
Transportation (self-driving cars)

Advantages:

Reduces human effort
Improves efficiency
Handles large data

Limitations:

High cost
Lack of creativity
Ethical concerns

Future Scope:
AI is expected to revolutionize industries like robotics, automation, and smart cities.

## Test Scenario 4: Role-Based Prompting

Prompt: “Act as a lecturer and explain Artificial Intelligence with real-life examples.”

Response

As your lecturer, think of Artificial Intelligence as teaching machines to behave intelligently. For example, when Netflix recommends movies, it uses AI to analyze your preferences. Similarly, Google Maps predicts traffic using AI. These systems learn from data and improve over time, just like students learning from experience.

## Test Scenario 5: Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Prompt: “Explain Artificial Intelligence step-by-step from basic ideas to advanced uses.”

Response
```
1.Basic Idea: AI begins with the concept of making machines mimic human thinking.
2.Data Input: Machines are trained using data.
3.Learning Process: Algorithms help systems learn patterns.
4.Decision Making: AI uses learned patterns to make decisions.
5.Advanced Uses: Applications include self-driving cars, medical diagnosis, and robotics
```

## Output
The experiment generated responses for six different prompting patterns applied to the topic Artificial Intelligence. The broad prompt produced a general and less detailed explanation with limited structure. The basic prompt improved clarity and made the response easier to understand, especially for beginners. The structured prompt provided a well-organized and comprehensive answer, covering all required aspects such as definition, types, applications, advantages, and future scope.
The role-based prompt enhanced engagement by explaining concepts using real-life examples, making the response more relatable. The chain-of-thought prompt delivered a step-by-step explanation, improving logical flow and depth of understanding. The comparative prompt generated a clear tabular comparison between Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning, showing high accuracy and structured presentation.
Overall, structured and guided prompts resulted in more accurate, detailed, and relevant outputs, while unstructured prompts produced weaker and less informative responses.

## Result
The experiment shows that structured and well-defined prompting patterns produce significantly better responses in terms of accuracy, clarity, and completeness compared to broad prompts.
